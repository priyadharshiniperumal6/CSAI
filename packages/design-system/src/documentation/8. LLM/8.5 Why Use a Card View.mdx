# Why Use a Card View

Cards are ideal for **browsing collections of self-contained items**. They provide a
visual summary and work well when content varies in length, type, or hierarchy.

## Use a Card View When

- Each item represents a **single concept** (record, asset, person, case).
- Users are **browsing or discovering** rather than comparing exact values.
- Content includes **mixed media** or variable metadata.
- You want **strong visual hierarchy** and scannability.
- The dataset is **small to medium** and not column-comparison heavy.

## Avoid Card Views When

- Users must **compare fields across many items**.
- Data is **high density** or primarily numeric.
- You need **bulk operations** across rows.

## Universal Theme Mapping

- Use `CardView` to render collections of cards.
- Use `UniCard` / `DataCard` for item summaries.
- Pair cards with filters or search when discovery is the goal.
